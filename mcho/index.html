How to Protect Yourself

1. Know How it Spreads


TOUCHING. COUGHING. SNEEZING

  • The best way to prevent COVID-19 is to avoid being exposed to this virus.

  • The virus spreads mainly from person-to-person:

  • Between people who are in close contact with one another (within about 6 feet).
  • Through respiratory droplets produced when an infected person coughs or sneezes.
  • These droplets can land in the mouths or noses of people who are nearby or possibly be inhaled into the lungs.


2. Steps to Protect Yourself


WASH YOUR HANDS.
AVOID TOUCHING.
SOCIAL DISTANCING.

  • Wash your hands often with soap and water for at least 20 seconds especially after you have been in a public place, or after blowing your nose, coughing, or sneezing.

  • Avoid touching your eyes, nose, and mouth with unwashed hands.

  • SOCIAL DISTANCING: Keep distance between yourself and strangers.

What to do if you are Sick

Emergency Warning Signs of COVID-19

If You Have The Following, Call Our Hotline for Medical Care:

  • Trouble breathing
  • Persistent pain or pressure in the chest
  • New confusion or inability to arouse
  • Bluish lips or face

Stay Home


If you do not have warning signs - Stay Home

  • Stay home from work, school, and away from other public places.

  • If you must go out, avoid using any kind of public transportation, ridesharing, or taxis.

  • Get rest and stay hydrated.

  • Separate yourself from other people in your home, this is known as home isolation

  • There is currently no vaccine to prevent coronavirus disease 2019 (COVID-19).

    The most common symptoms of COVID-19 are fever, tiredness and dry cough. Few patients experience aches and pains, nasal congestion, runny nose, sore throat or diarrhea.These symptoms may appear 2-14 days after exposure.

  • Who is at highest risk?

    Who are at High Risk?

    High Risk Patients Must Seek Immediate Medical Care


    • People aged 65 years and older.
    • Chronic lung disease or moderate to severe Asthma.
    • Cancer or immunocompromised including HIV patients.
    • Serious Heart Disease
    • Diabetes, renal failure, or liver disease.

    Older adults and people of any age who have serious underlying medical conditions might be at higher risk for severe illness from COVID-19.
